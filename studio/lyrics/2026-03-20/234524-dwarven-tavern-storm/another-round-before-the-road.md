# Another Round Before the Road

> Part of: Dwarven Tavern Storm -- Ale and the Road Home

---

## Approach

**Repetition-heavy drinking song** -- Built for a table of dwarves slamming tankards on the downbeat. The chorus is deliberately simple -- four lines, two of them repeated -- so anyone can join by the second pass. Verses tell the story, but the chorus owns the song. The bridge is the turn: the singing stops, the dwarf goes quiet, and the homesickness surfaces before the final chorus buries it in noise again. Heavy use of stomps, claps, and call-and-response. The repetition isn't lazy -- it's structural. Every "another round" is a delay tactic. He's drinking because the road isn't ready yet, and the song knows it.

### Style Block Notes

Best paired with a Celtic rock/folk arrangement at 126-130 BPM for maximum drinking-song energy. Bodhrán and stomping boots lock the rhythm -- heavy on beats 2 and 4 with handclaps layered. Fiddle plays a reel-style melody on chorus, acoustic guitar drives the verses with aggressive strumming. Male baritone vocal, hearty and big, talk-sung on verses and belted on choruses. Gang vocals essential on every chorus -- the more voices the better. G Major for warmth and singability. The bridge must drop to near-silence (solo acoustic guitar, maybe a distant tin whistle) so the final chorus explosion hits harder. ALL CAPS on the final chorus signals the vocal should push to a shout. The outro fiddle reel should feel like the pub celebrating his departure. Weirdness at 30-35% for accessibility. Style Influence at 65% for strong Celtic-pub character.
