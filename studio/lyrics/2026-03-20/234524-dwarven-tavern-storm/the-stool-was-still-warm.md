# The Stool Was Still Warm

> Part of: Dwarven Tavern Storm -- Ale and the Road Home

---

## Approach

**Narrative scene-by-scene** -- Each verse is a distinct camera shot: the storm door, the hearth, the ale, the window, the road. The dwarf doesn't explain how he feels -- the scenes do it for him. A blacksmith's hands wrapped around a tankard say more than "I'm tired." The storm clearing says more than "I'm ready." Concrete details carry the emotional weight -- soot under fingernails, foam on copper, the hiss of rain on hot stone. The chorus is the only place he names what's pulling him forward. Rising arc: restlessness builds verse by verse until the final verse breaks into motion.

### Style Block Notes

Best paired with a Celtic folk arrangement at 118-124 BPM. Fiddle carries the melody on intro and chorus, bodhrán provides the rhythmic drive. Acoustic guitar fingerpicked in verses, full strum on choruses. Tin whistle on the bridge solo -- airy and longing. Male baritone vocal, warm but gravelly, storytelling phrasing with breath between images. D Major for brightness with a wistful edge. Keep verses stripped (guitar + bodhrán) so the full-band chorus lifts. Stomps and claps enter on the final chorus for communal energy. The outro should feel like the door opening -- one sharp bodhrán hit, then silence. Weirdness at 40-45% to keep it grounded in folk tradition. Style Influence at 55-60% for strong Celtic character without over-stylizing.
