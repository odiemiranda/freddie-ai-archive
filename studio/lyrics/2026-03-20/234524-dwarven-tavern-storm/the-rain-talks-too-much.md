# The Rain Talks Too Much

> Part of: Dwarven Tavern Storm -- Ale and the Road Home

---

## Approach

**Conversational interior monologue** -- The dwarf is sitting alone, not performing for anyone. He's thinking out loud -- half to himself, half to the rain, half to nobody. Lines are short, fragmented, the way real thought works. No rhyme scheme because thoughts don't rhyme. The drinking song is happening at the next table; this is the quiet dwarf in the corner staring at his hands. The turn comes when the rain stops. Silence is louder than the storm, and in that silence he stands up. Sparse verses, no real chorus -- just a recurring line that shifts meaning each time. The emotional arc is flat brooding that cracks open into sudden, clear-eyed motion.

---

### Lyrics

```
[Intro]
[Rain on stone, single acoustic guitar, distant tavern murmur]

[Verse 1]
[Male baritone, low, almost spoken]
The rain talks too much
Keeps saying things I already know
Like "sit down" and "not yet"
and "who's waiting for you anyway"

Well... someone is
That's the whole point

[Verse 2]
[Tin whistle enters, distant]
My hands don't feel right
when they're empty
Spent the last forge
shaping a blade for a man
who won't swing it half as hard
as it deserves

That's the job though
Take the coin, walk the road
Find the next town
that needs something bent back into shape

[Verse 3]
[Acoustic guitar, gentle fingerpicking]
The ale's fine
Not saying it's bad
Just saying I've had better
from a barrel my wife tapped wrong
and we laughed about it
for a week

...That was autumn
It's almost spring now

[Break]
[Rain sounds, fire crackle, silence]

[Verse 4]
[Slightly more energy, bodhrán enters soft]
Funny thing about a storm --
you get used to the noise
It's the silence after
that gets you moving

The rain stopped

I didn't notice when

But the window's clear now
and there's a road out there
that I've been staring at
for three tankards

[Verse 5]
[Building, fiddle enters]
Right then

Coat's still wet but it'll dry walking
Pack's still heavy but it's lighter
every mile closer

The barkeep's wiping down my spot
before I've even stood up
Like he knew

[Outro]
[Full arrangement swells briefly, then cuts to footsteps on gravel]
Maybe he did

Everyone leaves this place eventually
The smart ones leave
before the next storm

[End]
```

### Style Block Notes

Best paired with a stripped Celtic folk arrangement at 110-114 BPM -- slower than the other two variations to match the interior pace. Acoustic guitar fingerpicked throughout, tin whistle as a distant color (not a lead), bodhrán enters only in verse 4 as the character wakes up. Male baritone vocal, low and conversational -- not singing so much as talking on pitch. Almost spoken-word in the early verses, gradually becoming more melodic as the character gains momentum. A Minor for the brooding quality, shifting toward D Major in the final verse for the lift. No gang vocals, no stomps -- this is a solo performance. The break should be genuinely quiet: rain ambience and fire crackle, nothing else. The outro swell is brief -- two bars of full arrangement that cuts sharply to footsteps. Weirdness at 50-55% for the unconventional structure. Style Influence at 45% to keep it loose and conversational rather than locked into folk tropes.
