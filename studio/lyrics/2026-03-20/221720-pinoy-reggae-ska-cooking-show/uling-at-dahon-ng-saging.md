# Uling at Dahon ng Saging

> Part of: Pinoy Reggae-Ska Cooking Show in the Province

---

## Approach

**Sensory-poetic, imagery-driven** -- Instead of listing dishes like a menu, this variation paints the dirty kitchen scene through smell, smoke, sound, and heat. Each verse is a sensory snapshot: the charcoal glow at dawn, the fat crackling in the kawali, steam rising off banana leaves. Dishes appear naturally inside the imagery rather than as callouts. The refrain "Uling at dahon ng saging" (charcoal and banana leaves) anchors every scene. Taglish with more Tagalog weighting. Medium density, with room for the reggae groove to breathe.

### Style Block Notes

Best paired with a reggae-leaning ska arrangement. Melodica lead for the Caribbean-Filipino crossover feel, offbeat guitar with a slightly rounder tone than pure ska. Walking bassline, one-drop influenced drums with ska hi-hat upbeats. Male vocal delivery should be warm and unhurried -- storytelling over bouncing, not shouting over it. 122 BPM, D Major for warmth and pride. Consider a melodica solo on the bridge and outro. Weirdness around 45-55% to allow some atmospheric texture. Style Influence at 50% for balanced reggae-ska feel.
