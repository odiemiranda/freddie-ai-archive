# Sigaw ng Barkada

> Part of: Galingan Natin

---

### Approach
**Repetition-heavy / Anthem** -- built for crowd singalong. Short punchy hook lines that lock into the melody fast. The chorus is a four-word mantra. Verses are compressed to 4 lines each, leaving maximum space for the hook to repeat. Gang shouts dominate. The volta hits in the bridge with a spoken moment of real talk before the final chorus erupts.

### Style Block Notes
- This variation lives and dies on the gang vocal energy -- Style should include `gang "oh-oh" sections`, `crowd chant`, `stacked backing vocals`
- Keep instruments simple: punchy drums, crunchy power chords, bass groove. The repetition in lyrics needs a tight, driving instrumental bed, not a busy one
- The spoken bridge needs dynamic contrast in the Style -- consider `breakdown` or `stripped instruments` before the build
- The `[Build]` into final chorus is critical -- Style should support this with `risers` or `tom fills`
- Do NOT duplicate "energetic" or "upbeat" in Style -- the ALL CAPS and "(HEY!)" already signal that to Suno
