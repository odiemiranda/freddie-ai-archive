# Grounds and Grounds

> Part of: Morning Coffee — Barista Cousins

---

### Approach

Hip-hop-leaning. Dense syllables, stacked internal rhyme, coffee wordplay running through packed lines. The double meaning of "grounds" — coffee grounds and the ground you stand on, your territory, your foundation — drives everything. Eight-line verses with 8-12 syllables per line, end rhyme on every couplet plus internal rhyme chains. This is the bars variation — rhythmic, confident, built to be rapped over a jazzy boom-bap beat. Sustained groove energy throughout, the way a good busy shift just flows.

### Style Block Notes

- This variation leans hardest into hip-hop. Boom-bap drums with swing should drive from the top — snare on 2 and 4, hi-hat with attitude, kick with weight.
- Piano should be stab-style jazz chords — think J Dilla or Madlib sample chops. Percussive, rhythmic, not flowing Rhodes.
- Bass needs to be round and present — upright with some grit, or warm electric bass with pocket. Not sub-heavy, but felt in the chest.
- The hook wants group vocal unison with punch — all four voices hitting "grounds" together.
- Bridge goes half-time — drums halve, bass holds long notes, piano sustains. This is the emotional turn before the final verse brings the energy back.
- Vinyl crackle in intro/outro bookends with that dusty boom-bap texture.
- Verse 3 should feel like the whole crew hitting their stride — full instrumentation, confident energy.
- A scratched turntable hit or horn stab between hook repetitions adds flavor without clutter.
- Avoid: auto-tune, trap hi-hats, anything too clean or modern. This should sound like it was recorded in the back room of the coffee shop.
