# First Pour

> Part of: Morning Coffee — Barista Cousins

---

### Approach

Jazz-leaning. Four-line verses with short, open phrases — written for a singer who needs room to bend notes, stretch vowels, and phrase around the beat. Each verse sketches one cousin in a few warm strokes, the way a jazz standard paints a whole scene in two lines. Loose slant rhyme, conversational phrasing, open vowels that sustain over chord changes. The chorus is a shared exhale. Arc rises gently from a quiet solo open to communal warmth — never pushes past a comfortable groove.

### Style Block Notes

- Rhodes or Wurlitzer carries the whole track — let it breathe, leave space between chords. Comping on offbeats, not filling every bar.
- Brushed drums or cross-stick snare only. No boom-bap, no heavy kick. This is jazz territory — the pocket lives in the swing, not the punch.
- Walking upright bass with room to roam. The bass and piano trade space, never step on each other.
- Vocals need air. The short lines are deliberate — Style should not fill the gaps with busy instrumentation. Let silence do work.
- Group vocals on chorus should be loose unison, not tight harmony. Think friends singing together, not a rehearsed group.
- A muted trumpet or breathy sax between sections adds jazz color. Keep it understated — one phrase, then out.
- Bridge strips to piano and voice only. Nothing else.
- Avoid: boom-bap drums, dense percussion, trap anything, fast hi-hats. This variation lives or dies on space and warmth.
