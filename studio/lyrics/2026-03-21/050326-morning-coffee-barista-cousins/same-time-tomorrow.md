# Same Time Tomorrow

> Part of: Morning Coffee — Barista Cousins

---

### Approach

Fusion midpoint — where jazz phrasing meets hip-hop groove. Six-line verses with moderate density, melodic enough to sing but rhythmic enough to bounce. Internal rhyme appears but doesn't dominate — it flavors the line without turning it into bars. The hook repeats like a daily ritual, deepening each time. Wave arc: builds in verses, settles into the chorus groove, builds again. The bridge is the emotional turn — stripped, honest, then the final chorus brings everyone back.

### Style Block Notes

- The groove here sits between jazz and hip-hop — syncopated drums with swing, not straight boom-bap and not pure brushed jazz. Think Dilla-influenced pocket with some softness.
- Rhodes comping on offbeats through verses, fuller chords on chorus. Let the keys breathe in the verse, fill in the chorus.
- Upright bass walks through the verses, locks into a groove pattern on the chorus. Warm tone, some natural grit.
- The hook phrase "same time tomorrow" repeats heavily — instrumentation should support it with a simple melodic pocket, not compete with it.
- Pre-chorus harmonies are the warmth escalator. Style should add a second voice or subtle horn pad here.
- Bridge thins to piano/keys and bass only. Vocal carries the emotion.
- Final chorus call-and-response needs group vocal layering — main voice leads, others answer in parenthetical lines.
- Finger snaps or shaker throughout keeps the groove human.
- Avoid: heavy sub bass, trap percussion, synth pads, reverb-heavy production. Keep it in the coffee shop.
