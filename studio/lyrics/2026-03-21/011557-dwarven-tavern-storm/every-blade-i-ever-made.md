# Every Blade I Ever Made

> Part of: Dwarven Tavern Storm -- Ale and the Road Home (Set 2)

---

## Approach

**Dense/rhythmic stomp** -- Built for a locked bodhrán groove with boot-stomps on every downbeat. Lines are packed tight with stressed syllables, plosive consonants, and short punchy phrases that hit like a hammer strike. The dwarf is a blacksmith -- he thinks in rhythm, in repetition, in the count of strikes per minute. Every verse is dense with concrete details: numbers, materials, physical actions. The lyrics lean trochaic (DUM-da DUM-da) to match a marching, stomping cadence. The rising arc comes from accumulation -- each verse stacks more weight until the final verse breaks the pattern. The dense rhythm suddenly goes sparse, the hammer stops, and the silence is the loudest part. The volta is structural: the pattern itself breaking is the turn.

---

### Lyrics

```
[Intro]
[Bodhrán count-in, boot stomps, tankard slam]

[Verse 1]
[Male baritone, driving, percussive delivery]
Fourteen blades in seven towns
Six-pound hammer, four-inch stock
Forged for hands that weren't my own
Coin in pocket, soot in lungs
Three weeks bent across the anvil
Sparks like copper, smoke like fog
Now I'm sitting in a tavern
Drinking ale that's good enough

[Verse 2]
[Fiddle riff enters, aggressive bowing]
Count the mugs across the table --
five men deep and all of them loud
Bread and bone broth, clinking copper
Laughter banked against the storm
The barkeep pours without me asking
Knows the look of "one more round"
Boot mud drying by the hearthstone
Pack still heavy, arms still sore

[Verse 3]
[Building, fuller arrangement]
Thirty miles to the next town over
Forty past that to the ridge
Sixty more to see the valley
Where my chimney cuts the sky
I've been mapping it in tankards --
One for thirty, two for forty
Three for sixty and the last one
That's for walking out the door

[Bridge]
[Half-time, bodhrán alone, slower]
Every blade I ever made
is swinging in a stranger's belt
Every hinge and every latch
is holding shut a stranger's door
I build things that hold for years
for people I won't see again

That's the job
But the job's not home

[Verse 4]
[Stripped -- no percussion, just acoustic guitar, sparse]
The storm quit

Didn't notice when

The room's still loud
but the rain's gone quiet
and there's a road out there
with my name on it

[Outro]
[Full band erupts for four bars, then cuts to solo footsteps]
Fourteen blades behind me
One forge ahead
```

### Style Block Notes

Best paired with a driving Celtic rock/folk arrangement at 124-130 BPM. The rhythm must be locked and relentless -- bodhrán hitting every beat, boot stomps layered, handclaps on 2 and 4. Fiddle plays riff-based lines, not flowing melodies -- short aggressive phrases that match the vocal density. Acoustic guitar in hard downstroke, almost percussive. Male baritone vocal, forceful and clipped, talk-sung with emphasis on stressed syllables. The bridge drops to half-time with solo bodhrán -- the breath before the break. Verse 4 must be genuinely stripped: solo acoustic guitar, no percussion, no fiddle. The silence after three dense verses is the emotional payload. The outro erupts for exactly four bars of full band (the decision made, the energy released) then cuts hard to footsteps. G Major for the driving verses, shifting to D Major for the bridge and final verse. Weirdness at 35-40% for accessibility. Style Influence at 60-65% for strong Celtic-pub-stomp character.
