# Still Here

> Part of: Dwarven Tavern Storm -- Ale and the Road Home (Set 2)

---

## Approach

**Emotion performance (director tags + vocal acting)** -- Uses Suno's director-tag system and punctuation-as-performance to force genuine vocal acting from the AI. The dwarf isn't narrating or drinking or stomping -- he's sitting with a tiredness that lives in his chest. Breathy, close-mic'd verses with hesitation built into the text through ellipses, stutters, and short fragmented lines. The contrast arc swings between intimate near-whisper and one raw, cracked-open moment where the homesickness surfaces as volume. The tavern atmosphere is warm and blurred -- sounds of people around him rendered as texture, not detail. The volta is self-directed: he realizes the storm ended a while ago, and it's him -- not the rain -- that's been keeping him in the chair.

### Style Block Notes

Best paired with a stripped, intimate Celtic folk arrangement at 100-110 BPM -- the slowest of the three variations to match the interior weight. Acoustic guitar fingerpicked throughout, close and warm. Tin whistle as a distant, almost-imagined color -- not a lead instrument. No percussion until the Build section, where bodhrán enters barely audible and grows. The single chorus is the emotional peak: full band for exactly those four lines, then immediately stripped back to near-silence. Male baritone vocal must sound close-mic'd and breathy -- like the singer is six inches from the microphone. The breathy quality is essential for the emotion tags to land. Stutters and ellipses in the text will guide Suno to natural hesitation if the vocal delivery is already intimate. A Minor for the verses (tight, interior), shifting to D Major for the chorus (the longing breaking open). The outro should feel like standing up slowly -- acoustic guitar simplifying to single notes, one held fiddle note, then nothing. Weirdness at 55-60% for the unconventional structure (one chorus in the whole song). Style Influence at 40-45% to keep it loose and human rather than polished.
