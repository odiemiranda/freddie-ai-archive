# Bus Stop Sunrise

> Part of: Coffee Half-Full

---

### Approach
Narrative jazzhop. Four-line verses with tighter rhythmic phrasing — same bus stop story but the bars land harder on the pocket. Moderate density with internal rhyme threading through each verse. Piano still leads but rides a chopped, swung drum loop. The volta keeps its punch: "Half is more than yesterday" — but the whole track sits deeper in the boom-bap pocket now.

### Lyrics
```
[Intro]
[Chopped piano loop, swung drums fade in]

[Verse 1]
Seven-fifteen, bus stop bench
Paper cup, the place on Third
Lid stained brown like a tiny crown
Two sips deep and the whole block heard

[Pre-Chorus]
Holding half a cup
Like it's holding me right back

[Chorus]
Coffee half-full, Tuesday morning

Half is more than yesterday

[Bridge]

[Chorus]
Coffee half-full, Tuesday morning
```

### Style Block Notes
- Piano-led but chopped — sample-style chords over a swung boom-bap drum loop
- Upright bass or warm sub bass sitting in the pocket, not walking
- Pre-chorus strips to voice and bass — held breath before the chorus opens
- Chorus gets the fullest arrangement but stays dusty — no clean/polished production
- Bridge: bass and finger snaps only, voice owns the space
- BPM sweet spot: 95-105, leaning into that Aso / Funky DL chillhop tempo
- Avoid synths, avoid anything polished — this is vinyl-warm, not studio-clean
- Think Tom Misch meets early Loyle Carner on a jazzhop sampler beat
