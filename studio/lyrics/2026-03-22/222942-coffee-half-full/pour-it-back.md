# Pour It Back

> Part of: Coffee Half-Full

---

### Approach
Hip-hop-leaning, hook-driven repetition. Six-line verses with denser syllable packing and internal rhyme. The hook phrase "pour it back" anchors every section -- meaning shifts from pouring coffee to pouring energy back into your day. The volta is built into the repetition: same phrase, new meaning each time.

### Lyrics
```
[Intro]
[Drums, bass pocket, Rhodes stab]
Yeah... yeah...

[Verse 1]
Alarm went off but I was already up
Kitchen light on, filling the cup
Coffee in the filter like a ritual now
Steam on my face like a quiet vow
Two sugars deep and the morning's mine
Half a cup left and it tastes just fine

[Hook]
Pour it back, pour it back
Morning coffee, morning track
Half-full cup and I'm all the way up
Pour it back, pour it back

[Verse 2]
Playlist on shuffle, something with brass
Feet on the cold tile, window got glass
Frosted at the edges but the sun's breaking through
Same kitchen, same mug, but the feeling is new
Neighbor's dog barking at a bird on the fence
Small things stacking and they all make sense

[Hook]
Pour it back, pour it back
Morning coffee, morning track
Half-full cup and I'm all the way up
Pour it back, pour it back

[Bridge]
[Stripped, just voice and snare]
I used to drain it to the dregs
Rush out the door on tired legs

'Cause half-full means I got enough

[Hook]
Pour it back, pour it back
Morning coffee, morning track

Pour it back -- yeah, pour it back

[Outro]
[Ad-libs over fading groove]
(Pour it back...)

```

### Style Block Notes
- This needs a hip-hop pocket -- boom bap or chillhop drums with swing
- Rhodes stabs (not sustained chords) to keep energy punchy
- Bass should be warm but rhythmic -- upright with plucked attack or Motown-style electric
- The hook needs to feel like a head-nod mantra -- keep production locked in the pocket
- Bridge strips to voice and a single snare hit per bar
- Ad-libs in the outro should feel natural, not scripted -- parentheses will trigger backing vocal layer
- BPM sweet spot: 95-100 for the hip-hop bounce
- Horn stabs (trumpet or sax) could accent the hook but should NOT be in every section
