# Two Voices at the Gate

> Part of: Celtic Tavern Ballad — The Forge & The Commander

---

### Approach
Conversational. The Commander dominates — measured, intimate, spoken-word lines that tell a story like someone recounting it years later at a fireside. Short declarative fragments. The Forge exists as texture: group humming under verses, single chanted lines between stanzas, never taking a full section until the final chorus. The wave arc peaks late — the Forge's full emergence in the last chorus is the climax. The volta is the role reversal: the Commander, who has been speaking alone, joins the Forge's chant at the end. He stops commanding and starts belonging.

### Lyrics
```
[Intro]
[Group humming, Deep, Distant]
Hmm... hmm... hmm...

[Verse 1]
[Spoken word, Deep baritone, Authoritative, Gravelly]
There were two of us at the gate.
One held a sword.
One held a torch.
[Spoken word, Deep baritone, Measured]
I don't remember which was mine.

[Interlude]
[Deep chanting baritone, Ancient, Quiet]
Stone remembers... stone remembers...

[Verse 2]
[Spoken word, Deep baritone, Gravelly, Intimate]
The wall was older than the war.
Older than the name they gave it.
We leaned against it...
like it owed us shade.

[Interlude]
[Deep chanting baritone, Ancient, Quiet]
Stone remembers... stone remembers...

[Chorus]
[Spoken word, Deep baritone, Commanding]
Hold the line.
[Building, Group chanting joins]
Hold the line.
[Powerful chanting, Deep]
Hold... the... line.

[Verse 3]
[Spoken word, Deep baritone, Stripped back, Intimate]
He told me once...
the mountain doesn't care who wins.
It just waits for the swords to rust
and the names to blur.
[Spoken word, Gravelly, Fading]
I think about that... more than I should.

[Bridge]
[Spoken word, Deep baritone, Vulnerable]
I stopped giving orders
the day I realized...
the best ones already knew.

[Final Chorus]
[Full chanting, Maximum power, Stomping, Ancient]
HOLD THE LINE
HOLD THE LINE
STONE REMEMBERS WHAT WE WERE
HOLD THE LINE
HOLD THE LINE

[Outro]
[Group humming, Fading into stone]
Hmm... hmm... hmm...
Hmm...
```

### Style Block Notes
- This is the most restrained variation — the Style should match. Sparse acoustic guitar or lute arpeggios under the Commander's spoken word. No full arrangement until the final chorus
- The "Stone remembers" interludes are the Forge as texture, not lead. They should sit low in the mix — deep chanting underneath, not on top. The Style should emphasize "distant" or "reverbed" qualities for these moments
- "Hold the line" chorus builds across three deliveries — spoken, then group, then full chant. The Style needs room for this dynamic arc. Don't put "powerful" or "anthemic" in Style tags — save that energy for the final chorus
- The final chorus is where both archetypes converge. ALL CAPS signals Suno to go full intensity. This is the payoff for three minutes of restraint
- Verse 3 is the longest — 5 lines with a trailing sixth. Folk genre allows this for storytelling verses (reference: 4-6 lines per verse). The extra line ("I think about that... more than I should") is the emotional weight — don't cut it
- Bodhrán should enter gradually. Silent or near-silent for verse 1, pulse only for verse 2, steady for chorus, full pattern for final chorus
- Gemini Review: 2 passes. Pass 1: tightened verse 2 line 2 ("Older than the name they gave it" — kept, concrete enough). Pass 2: no high-confidence issues. Deferred: Gemini suggested adding a pre-chorus before the final chorus — rejected (the bridge-to-final-chorus jump IS the point).
