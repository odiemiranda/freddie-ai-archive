# The Iron and the Word

> Part of: Celtic Tavern Ballad — The Forge & The Commander

---

### Approach
Narrative. The Commander owns the verses — short, declarative, spoken-word lines that set the scene with military brevity. The Forge owns the choruses — stomping AABB couplets with group chanting that turns the Commander's gravity into a tavern anthem. The wave arc builds through verse-chorus pairing, drops to a reverent spoken bridge, then erupts in a full-power final chorus. The volta lands at the bridge — the Commander, who has been giving orders, admits he's afraid too.

### Lyrics
```
[Intro]
[Group humming, Deep, Ancient]
Hmm... hmm... hmm...
Hmm... hmm... hmm...

[Verse 1]
[Spoken word, Deep baritone, Authoritative, Gravelly]
We left before the frost...
Forty men. Maybe less.
The road was mud and stone.
No one spoke about the rest.

[Pre-Chorus]
[Building, Group chanting joins, Deeper]
Down... down... the valley road
Down... where the river bends

[Chorus]
[Powerful chanting, Full group vocals, War drums pounding]
We forged the iron 'fore the sun
We hammered till the dawn was won
The mountain heard us speak her name
And NO man left the way he came

[Verse 2]
[Spoken word, Deep baritone, Gravelly, Stripped back]
The smithy at the crossroads...
Still had coals from the night.
A boy was sleeping near the door.
We didn't wake him. Felt right.

[Pre-Chorus]
[Building, Group chanting joins, Deeper]
Down... down... the valley road
Down... where the old stone stands

[Chorus]
[Powerful chanting, Full group vocals, War drums pounding]
We forged the iron 'fore the sun
We hammered till the dawn was won
The mountain heard us speak her name
And NO man left the way he came
SING! SING! SING!

[Bridge]
[Spoken word, Deep baritone, Stripped back, Intimate]
I told them... stand.
I told them... hold.
But the truth is...
[Spoken word, Gravelly, Vulnerable]
I was counting my own steps
to make sure I didn't run.

[Final Chorus]
[Full chanting, Maximum power, Stomping, Ancient]
WE FORGED THE IRON 'FORE THE SUN
WE HAMMERED TILL THE DAWN WAS WON
THE MOUNTAIN HEARD US SPEAK HER NAME
AND NO MAN LEFT THE WAY HE CAME
SING! SING! SING!

[Outro]
[Group humming, Fading into stone]
Hmm... hmm... hmm...
Hmm... hmm...
```

### Style Block Notes
- The Commander's verses need sparse instrumentation — bodhrán pulse and maybe a single acoustic guitar. Let the spoken word breathe. Adding too many instruments under spoken word drowns the delivery
- Forge choruses should hit with full bodhrán + stomping rhythm. The "SING!" caps lines push Suno toward shout delivery — confirmed in Forge testing
- The bridge is the quietest moment — strip back to drums only or even silence. The vulnerability contrast makes the final chorus explosion land
- "Down... down..." pre-choruses use ellipses to create descent — Suno reads these as hesitation/breath, which builds tension before the chorus
- Don't put "epic" or "heroic" in the Style — the ALL CAPS final chorus already signals that energy to Suno
- Gemini Review: 1 pass. Changed: tightened verse 2 line 3 from 9 syl to 8 ("A boy was sleeping near the door"). Deferred: none.
