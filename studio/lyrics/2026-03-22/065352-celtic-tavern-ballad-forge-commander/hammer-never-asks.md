# Hammer Never Asks

> Part of: Celtic Tavern Ballad — The Forge & The Commander

---

### Approach
Repetition-heavy. The Forge dominates — stomping AABB couplets built around a single anchor phrase ("the hammer never asks") that returns with shifting meaning each time. Group chanting layers in gradually, creating a wave that builds and releases. The Commander appears only at the bridge — his single spoken intervention breaks the rhythmic spell and reframes the chant. The volta is his arrival. Before the bridge, "the hammer never asks" sounds like strength. After the bridge, it sounds like grief. Sparse vocabulary, heavy repetition, trochaic stomp throughout.

### Style Block Notes
- This variation leans heavily on repetition — the Style should match with a steady, looping bodhrán pattern. No dynamic arrangement shifts until the bridge
- The Forge chanting should feel like a work song. Bodhrán + stomping rhythm is essential. Keep the tempo locked — 78-82 BPM, no rushing
- The Commander's bridge needs sudden contrast. Strip everything to spoken word with maybe a single drum pulse. The shift from full group chanting to one quiet voice is the emotional core
- After the bridge, the final chorus should feel different despite identical words — the ALL CAPS signals Suno to increase intensity, but the listener now hears "the hammer never asks" as loss, not strength
- Avoid tin whistle or fiddle melody here — this is a percussion-and-voice piece. The chant IS the melody
- Gemini Review: 1 pass. Changed: removed a fifth line from verse 2 that diluted the AABB structure. Deferred: none.
