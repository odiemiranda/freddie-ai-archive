# Steel and Stone

> Part of: Celtic Tavern — Dwarven Forge

---

### Approach
Repetition-heavy. The dwarven chanting ensemble owns this song — the anchor phrase "steel and stone" drives every chorus like a rhythmic mantra. The blacksmith only appears in sparse spoken verses between the chants, a man muttering into his ale while the choir thunders around him. The spiral arc returns to the anchor phrase three times, each time with shifted meaning: first pride, then loss, then the admission that the mountain remembers what he's trying to forget. The volta lands in the bridge — the mountain remembers, but he's the one who forgot.

### Style Block Notes
- Chanting sections need full bodhran + group vocals from the start — this is a chant-first song, not a verse-first song. The chorus hits before any spoken word
- Spoken verses should be near-silent underneath — maybe a single drone note or quiet bodhran pulse. The contrast between thundering chant and hushed spoken word is the whole architecture
- The bridge transition from group chanting to single voice is critical — "The mountain remembers" starts as choir, narrows to one voice, then the blacksmith speaks. Suno handles this if the bracket tags are clear
- "Forge-lit" and "torch-lit" are safe compound adjectives — Suno reads these as descriptive, not production instructions
- "Iron-sworn" in the chorus is a concrete oath-image, not abstract — it means sworn over iron, a dwarven vow
- Translated vibe words for Style: use "hushed" not "muted" for verse atmosphere, "resonant" not "distant" for the choir depth
