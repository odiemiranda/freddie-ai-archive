# Hammer and Dawn

> Part of: Celtic Tavern — Dwarven Forge

---

### Approach
Narrative. A dwarven blacksmith sits in a human tavern, far from the mountain halls, telling his story to anyone who'll listen. Verses are sparse and spoken — a man remembering. Choruses erupt into group chanting, the tavern singing along. The wave arc builds through verse-chorus pairing, drops to a quiet bridge where the real admission lands, then erupts in a full-power final chorus. The volta lands at the bridge — he says the mountain's too small now, but the truth is he outgrew it, or it outgrew him.

### Lyrics
```
[Short Instrumental Intro]
[Group humming, Deep, Ancient]
Hmm... hmm... hmm...
Hmm... hmm... hmm...

[Verse 1]
[Spoken word, Deep baritone, Gravelly]
His hands were wider than the anvil face.
My father. And his father before.
They'd start the forge at half past four
and the whole mountain shook by five.

[Pre-Chorus]
[Building, Group chanting joins, Deeper]
Down... down... the mine road
Down... where the torches burn in rows

[Chorus]
[Powerful chanting, Full group vocals, Stomping rhythm]
Hammer and dawn, hammer and dawn
We struck before the light came on
The mountain sang beneath our feet
And every blade knew forge-fire heat

[Verse 2]
[Spoken word, Deep baritone, Gravelly, Stripped back]
I left when the tunnels got thin.
Told myself the ceiling was too low.
Took a smith's kit and a copper ring
and walked until the sky got wide.

[Pre-Chorus]
[Building, Group chanting joins, Deeper]
Down... down... the mine road
Down... where the lanterns thinning glow

[Chorus]
[Powerful chanting, Full group vocals, Stomping rhythm]
Hammer and dawn, hammer and dawn
We struck before the light came on
The mountain sang beneath our feet
And every blade knew forge-fire heat
SING! SING! SING!

[Bridge]
[Spoken word, Deep baritone, Stripped back, Vulnerable]
The ceiling wasn't low...
The tunnels weren't too thin.
[Spoken word, Gravelly, Vulnerable]
Or maybe I outgrew them.
Or maybe they outgrew me.

[Final Chorus]
[Full chanting, Maximum power, Stomping, Ancient]
HAMMER AND DAWN, HAMMER AND DAWN
WE STRUCK BEFORE THE LIGHT CAME ON
THE MOUNTAIN SANG BENEATH OUR FEET
AND EVERY BLADE KNEW FORGE-FIRE HEAT
SING! SING! SING!

[Big Finish]
[Group humming, Fading into stone]
Hmm... hmm... hmm...
Hmm... hmm...
```

### Style Block Notes
- Verses need sparse instrumentation — bodhrán pulse, maybe a lone tin whistle. The spoken word needs air around it. Too many instruments under the voice drowns the delivery
- Forge choruses hit with full bodhrán + stomping rhythm + group vocals. The "SING!" caps lines push Suno toward shout delivery
- Pre-chorus "Down... down..." uses ellipses for breath/descent feel — Suno reads these as hesitation, building tension before the chorus
- Bridge is the quietest moment — strip to near-silence. The vulnerability contrast makes the final chorus explosion land harder
- Don't put "epic" or "heroic" in the Style — the ALL CAPS final chorus already signals that energy
- "Thinning" in the second pre-chorus is safe — it describes light fading, not a production instruction. Suno won't misread it in a lyric line
- Translated vibe words for Style block: use "unhurried" not "lazy" for verse pacing, "spacious" not "distant" for the bridge atmosphere
