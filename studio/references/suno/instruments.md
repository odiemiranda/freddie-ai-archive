## Golden Rule: Limit to 3-4 Instruments

More instruments ≠ better music. It equals sonic mud. Professional producers use restraint. Every additional instrument competes for space.

---

## Instrument Personality

Don't just name instruments—give them character:

| Basic | Professional |
|-------|-------------|
| "Piano" | "Dusty upright piano, honky-tonk charm" |
| "Guitar" | "Jangly 12-string, chimey and bright" |
| "Synth" | "Fat Moog bass, warm and growling" |
| "Drums" | "Lazy boom-bap, head-nodding groove" |

## TEXTURE & AMBIENT

### Lo-Fi Textures

```
Vinyl crackle - Record surface noise, nostalgic
Tape hiss - Cassette aesthetic, warm
Vinyl pops - Occasional clicks, authentic
Tape flutter - Warped playback, dreamy
Room tone - Natural ambience, present
```

### Nature Sounds

```
Rain sounds - Gentle rainfall, cozy
Thunder - Distant rumbles, dramatic
Ocean waves - Rhythmic surf, peaceful
Bird songs - Forest morning, natural
Flowing stream - Babbling brook, tranquil
Wind - Soft breeze, atmospheric
Fireplace crackle - Cozy warmth, intimate
```

### Ambient Textures

```
Synth pads - Sustained harmonic layers
Drones - Continuous tones, meditative
White/pink/brown noise - Sound masking
Binaural beats - Therapeutic frequencies
Field recordings - Real-world sounds
```

### Sound Design Elements

```
Risers - Building tension, transitions
Impacts - Dramatic hit moments, section changes
Whooshes - Cinematic movement, swooping energy
Sweeps - Frequency sweeping, filter movement
Noise layers - Textural bed, grit and atmosphere
```
