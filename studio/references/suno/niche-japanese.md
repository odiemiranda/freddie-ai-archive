# Japanese Aesthetic Niche Prompts

Battle-tested prompts for Japanese city pop, Studio Ghibli piano, anime OST, enka/kayokyoku, and Japanese lo-fi/zen.

> **Two Prompt Styles:** Each section includes **Style block prompts** (sonic palette with `[Instrumental]`/`[No Vocals]` bookends) and **natural-language prompts** (simple comma-separated descriptions). Structure tags (`[Intro]`, `[Build]`, `[Hook]`) go in the Lyrics block, not the Style block.

---

## JAPANESE AESTHETIC ZONE

### Japanese City Pop (80s Tokyo)

**Core Characteristics:**
- BPM: 105-125 (upbeat, groovy)
- Keys: E-flat Major, F Major, B-flat Major
- Elements: Funky bass, DX7 synth, Rhodes, jazzy guitar
- Atmosphere: Retro Tokyo nightlife, neon, bubble era
- Advanced Settings: Weirdness 25-45%, Style Influence 70-90% — city pop has a very specific sound; high style influence keeps it authentic. Vary: 25/85, 35/80, 45/70

**Prompts:**

```
[Instrumental] Japanese city pop, funky slap bass groove, vintage DX7 synth, Fender Rhodes chords, jazzy electric guitar, E♭ Major, 115 BPM, retro Tokyo nightlife, 80s neon aesthetic [No Vocals]
```

```
[Instrumental] 80s Tokyo city pop, smooth bass line, vintage synth, electric piano chords, jazzy guitar, F Major, 108 BPM, retro warmth, city pop nostalgia [No Vocals]
```

```
[Instrumental] City pop funk, slap bass rhythm, vintage synthesizer layers, warm Rhodes piano, clean guitar comping, B♭ Major, 120 BPM, bubble era Tokyo, sophisticated retro [Fade Out] [No Vocals]
```

```
[Instrumental] Japanese retro pop, funky bass groove, DX7 synth leads, electric piano chords, jazzy guitar melody, E♭ Major, 112 BPM, neon Tokyo nights, 80s nostalgia [No Vocals]
```

### Studio Ghibli Piano Style

**Core Characteristics:**
- BPM: 70-95 (gentle, flowing)
- Keys: C Major, F Major, G Major
- Elements: Solo piano, gentle melody, innocent atmosphere
- Atmosphere: Whimsical, nostalgic, magical realism
- Advanced Settings: Weirdness 20-40%, Style Influence 75-90% — Ghibli has a very recognizable feel; stay close to the target. Vary: 20/90, 30/80, 40/75

**Prompts:**

```
[Instrumental] Ghibli-inspired piano, gentle solo melody, nostalgic harmonies, innocent atmosphere, C Major, 80 BPM, whimsical childhood memories, heartwarming and magical [No Vocals]
```

```
[Instrumental] Studio Ghibli style, tender piano solo, flowing nostalgic melody, innocent harmonies, F Major, 75 BPM, magical realism, childhood wonder [No Vocals]
```

```
[Instrumental] Whimsical piano piece, gentle melodic lines, nostalgic chord progressions, innocent and pure, G Major, 88 BPM, Ghibli magic, heartwarming memories [Fade Out] [No Vocals]
```

```
[Instrumental] Nostalgic Ghibli piano, soft solo melody, tender harmonies, innocent atmosphere, C Major, 82 BPM, whimsical childhood, magical heartwarming [No Vocals]

### Japanese Lo-Fi & Zen

**Core Characteristics:**
- BPM: 70-82 (gentle, meditative groove)
- Keys: A Minor, G Major, D Minor
- Elements: Koto or shakuhachi samples, soft boom-bap drums, warm bass, vinyl texture
- Atmosphere: Zen garden, cherry blossom, eastern tranquility
- Advanced Settings: Weirdness 40-60%, Style Influence 50-70% — fusion genre benefits from moderate experimentation. Vary: 40/65, 50/55, 60/45

**Prompts:**

```
[Instrumental] Japanese lo-fi, koto samples, soft boom-bap drums, warm bass, sakura atmosphere, A Minor, 78 BPM, peaceful zen garden vibes, eastern tranquility [No Vocals]

```

```

## ANIME & GAME OST

### Anime Soundtrack (Non-Ghibli)

**Core Characteristics:**
- BPM: 80-140 (varies widely by mood — emotional scenes slower, battle/action faster)
- Keys: A Minor, D Minor (dramatic), C Major, G Major (hopeful)
- Elements: Orchestral strings, piano, electric guitar, driving percussion
- Atmosphere: Cinematic, emotional, narrative-driven, dynamic contrast
- Advanced Settings: Weirdness 35-55%, Style Influence 55-75% — anime OSTs have recognizable tropes but benefit from some surprise. Vary: 35/75, 45/65, 55/55

**Tagged Prompts:**

```
[Instrumental] Anime emotional scene, soft piano, emotional strings, cinematic orchestral, A Minor, 85 BPM, bittersweet farewell atmosphere, powerful and sweeping [Fade Out] [No Vocals]
```

```
[Instrumental] Anime battle theme, driving electric guitar, intense orchestral strings, pounding drums, B Minor, 140 BPM, high-energy confrontation, epic and urgent [No Vocals]
```

```
[Instrumental] Anime slice-of-life, warm acoustic guitar, light piano, soft percussion, G Major, 105 BPM, school rooftop afternoon, nostalgic and carefree [No Vocals]
```

```
[Instrumental] Anime opening energy, J-rock influence, electric guitar riff, punchy drums, synth accents, A Minor, 130 BPM, dynamic and exciting, youth energy [No Vocals]
```

**Natural-Language Prompts:**

```
Anime emotional piano, slow orchestral build, 85 BPM, A Minor, bittersweet mood, cinematic and tender
```

```
Anime battle music, intense orchestra, electric guitar, 140 BPM, B Minor, epic confrontation
```

```
Anime slice-of-life, warm acoustic guitar, gentle piano, 105 BPM, G Major, afternoon mood, nostalgic
```

```
Anime ending theme, soft and reflective, piano melody, gentle strings, 80 BPM, A Minor, peaceful resolution
```

## ENKA & KAYOKYOKU

### Enka (Modern Traditional Ballad)

**Core Characteristics:**
- BPM: 60-85 (slow, dramatic)
- Keys: A Minor, D Minor, E Minor (Japanese minor pentatonic feel)
- Elements: Shamisen or koto accents, orchestral strings, tremolo guitar, dramatic vocal space
- Atmosphere: Melancholic, dramatic, deeply emotional, traditional
- Advanced Settings: Weirdness 20-40%, Style Influence 75-90% — enka is a very specific genre; high style influence is essential. Vary: 20/90, 30/80, 40/75

**Tagged Prompts:**

```
[Instrumental] Enka ballad, dramatic orchestral strings, koto accents, tremolo guitar, A Minor, 72 BPM, deeply emotional, traditional Japanese drama, melancholic beauty [No Vocals]
```

```
[Instrumental] Modern enka, solo koto, lush strings, shamisen accents, soft percussion, D Minor, 68 BPM, longing, heartbreak, rain-soaked evening [No Vocals]
```

```
[Instrumental] Enka orchestral, sweeping strings, traditional Japanese scales, dramatic dynamics, E Minor, 78 BPM, emotional peak and tender resolve, theatrical beauty [No Vocals]
```

**Natural-Language Prompts:**

```
Enka instrumental, dramatic strings, koto, 72 BPM, A Minor, deeply emotional Japanese ballad style
```

```
Modern enka, orchestral arrangement, traditional melody, 68 BPM, D Minor, melancholic and beautiful, cinematic
```

### Kayokyoku (60s-70s Japanese Pop)

**Core Characteristics:**
- BPM: 95-120 (moderate pop tempo)
- Keys: C Major, F Major, A Minor
- Elements: Clean electric guitar, organ, brass accents, retro production
- Atmosphere: Retro charm, romantic, sophisticated, pre-city-pop warmth
- Advanced Settings: Weirdness 25-45%, Style Influence 70-85% — era-specific genre needs tight control. Vary: 25/85, 35/75, 45/70

**Tagged Prompts:**

```
[Instrumental] 1970s kayokyoku, clean electric guitar, warm organ chords, light brass accents, C Major, 108 BPM, retro Japanese pop charm, romantic and nostalgic [No Vocals]
```

```
[Instrumental] Kayokyoku style, vintage production, gentle strings, jazzy guitar, soft drums, F Major, 100 BPM, 1960s Tokyo romance, sophisticated retro [No Vocals]
```

```
[Instrumental] Showa era pop, warm analog production, brass melody, clean guitar, soft percussion, A Minor, 112 BPM, vintage Japanese charm, bittersweet nostalgia [No Vocals]
```

**Natural-Language Prompts:**

```
Kayokyoku, 1970s Japanese pop, clean guitar, organ, brass, 108 BPM, C Major, retro romantic mood
```

```
Showa era pop instrumental, vintage production, 100 BPM, F Major, warm and nostalgic, sophisticated charm
```

## J-ROCK & VISUAL KEI

### J-Rock

**Core Characteristics:**
- BPM: 120-160 (driving energy)
- Keys: E Minor, A Minor, B Minor
- Elements: Distorted electric guitar, driving drums, bass, occasional piano
- Atmosphere: Passionate, energetic, emotional intensity
- Advanced Settings: Weirdness 35-55%, Style Influence 55-75% — J-rock has distinctive energy but room for variation. Vary: 35/75, 45/65, 55/55

**Tagged Prompts:**

```
[Instrumental] J-rock, driving distorted guitar, powerful drums, melodic bass, E Minor, 145 BPM, passionate energy, anime opening intensity [No Vocals]
```

```
[Instrumental] J-rock ballad, emotional electric guitar, soft drums, full band, A Minor, 125 BPM, rooftop sunset, bittersweet intensity [No Vocals]
```

```
[Instrumental] Post-rock J-rock fusion, layered guitars, tremolo picking, building drums, atmospheric pads, B Minor, 135 BPM, cinematic and expansive, emotional crescendo [No Vocals]
```

**Natural-Language Prompts:**

```
J-rock instrumental, distorted guitar, driving drums, 145 BPM, E Minor, passionate and intense
```

```
J-rock ballad, emotional guitar, slow build to powerful climax, 125 BPM, A Minor, bittersweet mood
```

> **Tip:** Japanese niches span a huge BPM range (65 BPM enka to 160 BPM J-rock). Always match BPM to the specific sub-genre. See niche-specific sweet spots in `production.md`.
