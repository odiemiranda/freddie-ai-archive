# Track Naming Guide

Track names should feel like **fragments of real life** — a feeling caught mid-thought, not a scene described in full. Don't narrate. Evoke.

**Core principle:** Study the Style and Lyrics, then find the **shortest phrase that carries the feeling**. Say it out loud — if it feels good in the mouth, it's a name.

## Word Count Gravity

**1-3 words is the default.** Most of the 18 names should live here. Longer names (4-5 words) are occasional. 6+ words should be rare and only when the length is the point (the joke, the ramble, the breath).

- Drop articles when possible: "Storm" not "The Storm". "Road Home" not "The Road Home"
- Drop verbs when the noun carries it: "Still Warm" not "It Was Still Warm"
- Fragments over sentences: "Almost Home" not "I Was Almost Home"

## Naming Strategies

The `tracknames` tool generates 18 names (6 per model). Each model brings different naming instincts — MiniMax leans poetic, Gemini leans analytical, Grok leans playful.

| Strategy | Description | Examples |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Object as title** | One or two concrete nouns, no verb | "Old Iron", "Stone and Ale", "Copper Light" |
| **Fragment** | Incomplete thought, no verb needed | "Almost Home", "Before the Pass", "Not Yet" |
| **Contrast pair** | Two words that create tension | "Warm Dark", "Iron Rain", "Slow Fire" |
| **Simple ritual** | 2-3 word everyday moments | "Morning Coffee", "Stained Glass", "Last Round" |
| **Slice-of-life** | A small relatable thing (keep short) | "Wrong Bus", "Cold Mug", "Missed Stop" |
| **Inner thought** | Something you'd think but not say | "Almost Said It", "Not This Time" |
| **Self-aware humor** | Gently funny, can run longer | "No Thoughts, Just Groove", "I Forgot Why I Stood Up" |
| **Quiet observation** | Noticing something small | "Steam Stopped", "Window Left Open" |
| **Sound/texture word** | A single word chosen for mouth-feel | "Hollow", "Rust", "Soot" |
| **Mood caption** | Caption for a feeling, 2-3 words max | "Still Here", "Good Tired", "Almost Light" |

## Rules

1. Each set of 18 must use a mix of strategies across the three models
2. No recycled words within a track folder
3. **Default short (1-3 words), occasional medium (4-5), rare long (6+)**
4. Study the variation first — read Style and Lyrics, then find the fitting feeling
5. Say it out loud — the name should have rhythm even without the song
6. Conversational over poetic — "Still Warm" beats "Lingering Warmth of Yesterday"
7. No music-description titles — name the feeling, not the sound
8. No full sentences — fragments, not narration

## Anti-Patterns

- **Full sentences** ("The Man Who Walked Through Every Storm") — find the fragment ("Every Storm")
- **`[Adj] [Noun] [Noun]` formula** — sounds AI-generated ("Ethereal Whisper of Dawn")
- **Describing instruments** ("Harp and Whistle Evening") — find the feeling
- **Overused mood words** (drift, haze, glow, ethereal, whisper)
- **All names same length** — if all 18 are 4-5 words, something's wrong
- **Articles everywhere** — "The" at the start of most names signals lazy naming

## Fatigue Words (actively avoid)

**Nouns:** whisper, echo, shadow, mist, haze, glow, drift, shimmer, ember, silhouette, reverie, solace
**Adjectives:** gentle, ethereal, distant, fading, misty, hazy, golden, velvet, moonlit, serene, tender

**Instead:** verbs, specific objects, time references, mundane things, single texture words, incomplete thoughts.
