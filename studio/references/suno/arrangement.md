# Arrangement Reference

Instrument frequency registers and genre contribution mapping for fusion and mix decisions.

## Instrument Frequency Register

Use during analysis to check whether instruments overlap. Two instruments in the same band competing for the same role = mud.

| Register | Frequency Range | Instruments |
|----------|----------------|-------------|
| **Sub-bass** | 20-80 Hz | 808 bass, sub synth, bass drops |
| **Low** | 80-250 Hz | Upright bass, cello, bass guitar, tuba, low piano, warm bass synth |
| **Low-mid** | 250 Hz-1 kHz | Acoustic guitar (body), Rhodes/keys (fundamental), harp (low strings), bodhrán, warm pads |
| **Mid** | 1-4 kHz | Vocals (fundamental), fiddle, mandolin, accordion, trumpet, sitar, erhu, banjo |
| **High-mid** | 4-8 kHz | Shamisen, tin whistle, flute, ukulele, harp (high strings), bright piano, steel guitar |
| **High/Air** | 8 kHz+ | Hi-hats, shakers, vinyl crackle, cymbal shimmer, triangle, chimes, sparkle effects |

**Rules of thumb:**
- Two melodic instruments in the same register = they fight. Assign one to melody, the other to harmony/arpeggios, or cut one
- Rhythm instruments can share a register if they alternate (e.g., kick and bass don't play at the same time)
- Texture elements (crackle, rain, shimmer) can layer in high/air without conflict
- If bass + low-mid are both thick/warm, the mix muds. Add a bright element (hi-hat, crackle) for separation

## Genre Contribution Map

What each genre *brings* to a fusion. If two genres contribute the same element, one should yield.

| Genre Family | Primary Contribution | Secondary | What It Brings to a Fusion |
|-------------|---------------------|-----------|---------------------------|
| **Lo-fi** | Texture | Mood | Dust, vinyl crackle, tape warmth, imperfection. The *filter* over everything |
| **Hip-hop** | Rhythm | Bass | Boom-bap or trap drums, 808 bass, groove. Rhythmic backbone |
| **Jazz** | Harmony | Melody | Complex chords (7ths, 9ths, 13ths), swing, improvisation spaces |
| **Reggae** | Rhythm | Mood | Offbeat guitar chop, one-drop drums, laid-back groove |
| **Celtic/Folk** | Melody | Texture | Fiddle/tin whistle melodies, modal scales, organic warmth |
| **EDM/Electronic** | Rhythm | Texture | Four-on-the-floor, synth textures, builds/drops |
| **Ambient** | Texture | Mood | Pads, reverb, space, drones. Fills negative space without competing |
| **R&B/Soul** | Melody | Harmony | Vocal-forward melodies, smooth grooves, emotional delivery |
| **Rock** | Energy | Rhythm | Distorted guitars, driving drums, intensity |
| **Classical/Cinematic** | Melody | Texture | Orchestral arrangements, dynamic range, sweeping emotion |
| **Latin** | Rhythm | Melody | Clave patterns, percussion variety, syncopation |
| **Country/Bluegrass** | Melody | Texture | Acoustic string melodies (banjo, steel guitar) |
| **Punk** | Energy | Rhythm | Speed, aggression, raw production |
| **World/Global** | Melody | Texture | Unique timbres/scales (shamisen, sitar, erhu, kora) |

**Fusion conflict patterns:**
- **Two rhythm genres** (hip-hop + reggae): One must lead the groove, the other adds flavor
- **Two melody genres** (Celtic + World): Instruments fight for spotlight. Assign lead vs. accompaniment
- **Texture + anything**: Lo-fi, ambient, production textures layer without conflict — safest fusion partners
- **Energy vs. chill** (punk + lo-fi): Needs clear structure — punk energy in chorus, lo-fi chill in verse
