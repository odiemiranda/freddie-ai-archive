# The Forge

> Deep chanting baritone — dwarven hall resonance, Norse drinking song, ancient and earthy

---

| Attribute | Value |

## Lyrics Technique

### Vocal direction tags (use before specific lines)
```
[Deep chanting baritone, Ancient, Rhythmic stomp]          ← verses, steady march
[Building, Group chanting joins, Deeper]                    ← pre-chorus, voices swelling
[Powerful chanting, Full group vocals, War drums pounding]  ← chorus, hall-shaking power
[Deep baritone, Storytelling, Ancient cadence]              ← second verse, lore delivery
[Spoken word, Deep baritone, Reverent, Drums only]          ← bridge, honoring the mountain
[Full chanting, Maximum power, Stomping, Ancient]           ← final chorus, everything
[Group humming, Fading into stone]                          ← outro, echoing away
```

### Writing rules
- **Chanting over singing** — this voice chants in rhythmic cadence, not melodic lines. Steady syllable patterns that lock with the stomp
- **Group humming bookends** — "Hmm... hmm... hmm..." opens and closes the song. The sound of voices echoing through stone halls
- **AABB couplet rhyme** — "We carved the stone before the sun / We forged the iron, every one" — ancient poetry demands clean couplets, not slant rhyme
- **ALL CAPS on "SING"** — the chorus command word gets shouted. Final chorus goes full caps
- **Pre-chorus as descent** — "Down... down... to the roots we go" — ellipses and repetition create the feeling of going deeper
- **Bridge as reverence** — spoken word, deep and slow. The chanting stops for a moment of honor. The mountain is alive
- **Forge and stone imagery** — anvils, hammers, iron, gold veins, carved stone, underground rivers. Everything is craft and earth
- **No modern language** — "e'er" not "ever", "our fathers' fathers" not "our ancestors". The vocabulary feels old without being unreadable
- **Stomping rhythm in the writing** — lines should have a natural 4-beat stomp pattern. Read them while tapping your foot — if they don't lock, rewrite

### Line examples
```
We carved the stone before the sun
We forged the iron, every one
The mountain knew us by our name
Before the kings of men e'er came
```

```
They say the mountain sleeps...
But we hear her breathing
In every crack... every vein of gold
She remembers... and so do we
```

## Best Paired With

| Genre | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Epic folk | War drums + bodhran + lute is the natural habitat |
| Viking metal (clean) | Heavy drums and group chanting without screaming |
| Celtic | Bodhran and acoustic instruments support the ancient feel |
| Cinematic fantasy | Orchestral elements amplify the underground hall atmosphere |

## Advanced Settings

| Setting | Value | Reason |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Weirdness | 35-45% | Moderate — chanting needs a steady rhythmic pattern, not experimental drift |
| Style Influence | 60-70% | High influence locks the deep group chanting and prevents drift into clean melodic singing |

## Tested

- 2026-03-22: Epic folk, D Minor, 78 BPM — deep group chanting confirmed. Stomping rhythm and war drums anchored the primal feel. "SING" in caps pushed chorus to shout delivery. Bridge reverent spoken word contrasted perfectly before final ALL CAPS explosion. Group humming bookends echoed through stone halls naturally. High Style Influence (65%) kept chanting character locked.
