# The MC

> Rhythmic mid-range spoken word — words as drums, percussive cadence, bars over beats

---

## Voice Profile

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Register** | Mid-range |
| **Tone** | Sharp, confident, street-smart |
| **Delivery** | Percussive, rhythmic, every syllable placed with intent |
| **Energy** | High focus — controlled intensity, never sloppy |
| **Pacing** | Tight and measured, speeds up for verses, slows for emphasis on bridge |

## Style Tags

```
mid-range male vocals, rhythmic spoken word delivery, percussive cadence, words hit like drums, confident and sharp, street-smart energy
```

## Exclude Style

```
singing, melodic, soft, gentle, auto-tune, trap, electronic, female vocals, orchestral, reverb-heavy
```

## Lyrics Technique

### Vocal direction tags (use before specific lines)
```
[Spoken word, Rhythmic, Confident]                        ← intro, setting the stage
[Rhythmic delivery, Percussive, Sharp]                    ← verses, bars landing on beats
[Rhythmic delivery, Faster, More intense]                 ← second verse, escalation
[Building, Tighter flow]                                  ← pre-chorus, syllable stacking
[Powerful vocals, Punchy, Gang vocal echo]                ← chorus, maximum impact
[Spoken word, Slower, Deliberate, Stripped back]           ← bridge, getting real
[Spoken word, Confident, Fading]                          ← outro, last word
```

### Writing rules
- **Words as percussion** — every syllable lands on a beat. Lines should have natural rhythmic stress patterns that lock with the drum groove
- **Internal rhyme over end rhyme** — "write bars on the back of receipts / fold 'em twice, put 'em back in my jeans" — rhyme WITHIN lines, not just at the ends
- **Stacked multi-syllable rhyme** — "receipts/jeans", "proof/roof", "light/twice" — dense rhyme schemes show craft
- **Short punchy chorus lines** — 4-6 syllables max. The chorus is a statement, not a verse
- **Gang vocal echoes** — parenthesized responses: "(don't lie!)", "(don't sleep!)" — crowd confirmation
- **ALL CAPS for final chorus only** — the energy builds across the song, peaks at the end
- **Bridge as honesty** — drop the bravado for one moment. Spoken word, slower, deliberate. The MC being real about why they do this
- **Pre-chorus as rhythmic build** — repetitive syllable stacking: "Stack 'em up, stack 'em up" — creates momentum into the chorus
- **Concrete craft imagery** — receipts, notebooks, pens, pages. The MC talks about writing, not about being famous

### Line examples
```
I write bars on the back of receipts
Fold 'em twice, put 'em back in my jeans
Every line is a brick in the wall
Every word is a step, never crawl
```

```
People ask me why I do this
Like there's something else I could be doing
Nah... this is the only thing
That sounds like me when I hear it back
```

## Best Paired With

| Genre | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Boom bap | Snare-heavy beats and vinyl bass are the natural home |
| Lo-fi hip-hop | Dusty beats support the rhythmic delivery |
| Jazz rap | Live instrumentation adds sophistication to the flow |
| Spoken word | Stripped arrangement puts full focus on the cadence |

## Advanced Settings

| Setting | Value | Reason |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Weirdness | 40-50% | Moderate — needs room for flow variations but shouldn't drift into experimental territory |
| Style Influence | 50-60% | Balanced — too high locks the rhythm too tight, too low loses the percussive delivery |
