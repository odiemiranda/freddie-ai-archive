# The Crooner

> Silky low-mid baritone — late-night lounge, romantic velvet, effortless delivery

---

## Voice Profile

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Register** | Low-mid baritone |
| **Tone** | Silky, velvet, warm, smooth |
| **Delivery** | Effortless, behind the beat, romantic |
| **Energy** | Low — never strains, never hurries. Everything sounds easy |
| **Pacing** | Laid-back, slightly behind the beat, letting phrases linger |

## Lyrics Technique

### Vocal direction tags (use before specific lines)
```
[Smooth baritone, Silky, Effortless]                ← verses, main delivery
[Warm crooning, Velvet vocals, Slightly lifted]      ← chorus, gentle emotional lift
[Smooth baritone, Intimate, Behind the beat]          ← second verse, deeper intimacy
[Spoken word, Low baritone, Close-mic, Tender]        ← bridge, quiet confession
[Soft vocals, Fading, Humming over bass]              ← outro, dissolving into the room
```

### Writing rules
- **Behind the beat** — lines should feel like they arrive a half-second late, unhurried. Short phrases with space between them
- **Ellipses for lingering** — "You're still here... and so am I" — not dramatic pauses, just letting the moment sit
- **Small romantic details** — melted ice, a smile caught, a bartender who knows your name. Intimate, not grand
- **No big declarations** — the Crooner never says "I love you." He says "What's the hurry, honestly"
- **Slant rhyme only** — glass/ask, name/unmasked. Perfect rhyme sounds too eager for this voice
- **Lines under 9 syllables** — keeps the effortless feel, prevents rushing
- **Chorus as invitation** — "Stay a little longer" — the chorus is a gentle ask, not an anthem
- **Humming for outros** — same as the Storyteller but slower, more melodic, dissolving into the arrangement

### Line examples
```
The ice has melted in your glass
The band is down to three
But you're still here... and so am I
So what's the hurry, honestly
```

```
You know... the best nights
Never start with a plan
```

## Best Paired With

| Genre | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Smooth jazz | Brushed drums + walking bass is the natural habitat |
| Bossa nova | Light rhythm matches the effortless delivery |
| Lo-fi jazz | Vinyl warmth adds nostalgic texture |
| R&B ballad | Slow groove supports the romantic tone |

## Advanced Settings

| Setting | Value | Reason |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Weirdness | 20-30% | Voice needs to stay smooth and controlled, no surprises |
| Style Influence | 65-75% | High influence locks the velvet tone and prevents drift into scatting or runs |
