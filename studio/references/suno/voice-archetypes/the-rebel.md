# The Rebel

> Raspy mid-range snarl — garage rock defiance, raw punk energy, sweat and attitude

---

## Voice Profile

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Register** | Mid-range |
| **Tone** | Raspy, gritty, slightly distorted, raw |
| **Delivery** | Snarling, defiant, cocky, sneering |
| **Energy** | High — loud, unapologetic, sweat-on-the-mic |
| **Pacing** | Driving, punchy, words spit out not sung |

## Lyrics Technique

### Vocal direction tags (use before specific lines)
```
[Raspy vocals, Defiant, Sneering]                        ← verses, cocky delivery
[Gritty, Building, Aggressive]                            ← pre-chorus, ramp to explosion
[Raw vocals, Snarling, Full band, Explosive]              ← chorus, full defiance
[Spoken word, Raspy, Quiet for one second, Sneering]      ← bridge, the sneer before the storm
[Raw vocals, Screaming, Full band, Maximum energy]        ← final chorus, all caps, peak
```

### Writing rules
- **ALL CAPS for the hook** — "I DON'T CARE" — the Rebel shouts, not sings. Final chorus goes full caps for maximum Suno aggression
- **Short punchy lines** — 6-8 syllables max. This voice spits, doesn't flow
- **Concrete rebellion details** — amps at eleven, notes from landlords, windows left open. Specific acts of defiance, not abstract anger
- **Pre-chorus as taunt** — two lines, direct, confrontational: "You want quiet? Wrong house"
- **Bridge drops to a sneer** — quiet spoken word for ONE moment before the final explosion. The contrast makes the last chorus hit harder
- **Exclamation marks earn their place** — use on chorus hooks and final chorus only. Overuse dulls the impact
- **No vulnerability** — the Rebel doesn't reflect, regret, or soften. Save that for The Whisper
- **Humor through cockiness** — "I taped it to the kick drum / Now it rattles back at me" — the Rebel is funny because he doesn't care, not because he's trying

| Grunge | Gritty tone with dynamic quiet-loud contrast |
| Hardcore | Maximum aggression for the final chorus explosion |

## Advanced Settings

| Setting | Value | Reason |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Weirdness | 50-60% | High weirdness gives raw energy room to breathe and find unexpected pockets |
| Style Influence | 40-50% | Lower influence lets the punk attitude find itself — too controlled kills the energy |

## Tested

- 2026-03-21: Garage rock, E Major, 138 BPM — raspy snarl confirmed without drifting into metal scream. Bridge quiet sneer contrast worked — dropped to spoken word then final chorus exploded in ALL CAPS. Higher Weirdness (55%) was key to letting the raw energy feel spontaneous.
