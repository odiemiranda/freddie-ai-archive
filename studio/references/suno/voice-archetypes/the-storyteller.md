# The Storyteller

> Warm mid-baritone narrator — campfire tales, folk intimacy, unhurried conversation

---

## Voice Profile

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Register** | Mid baritone |
| **Tone** | Warm, gentle, intimate, close-mic |
| **Delivery** | Conversational, unhurried, storytelling |
| **Energy** | Low-moderate — never pushes, never whispers |
| **Pacing** | Slow and natural, pauses between thoughts like remembering |

## Style Tags

```
warm mid-baritone male vocals, gentle storyteller delivery, unhurried and conversational, intimate and close, analog warmth
```

## Exclude Style

```
electronic, synth, female vocals, auto-tune, heavy drums, distortion, falsetto, belting, fast tempo
```

## Lyrics Technique

### Vocal direction tags (use before specific lines)
```
[Warm baritone, Conversational, Unhurried]        ← verses, main narration
[Gentle, Warm vocals, Slightly fuller]              ← chorus, emotional lift without belting
[Spoken word, Warm, Close-mic, Reflective]          ← bridge, intimate moments
[Humming, Warm, Fading]                             ← outro, wordless warmth
```

### Writing rules
- **Conversational phrasing** — write like someone remembering, not performing. "There was a road I used to walk" not "Upon the road I wandered forth"
- **Ellipses for memory pauses** — "I never asked... I think she knew" — the pause is the storyteller gathering the next thought
- **Concrete small details** — tomatoes, old chairs, fences. The Storyteller lives in specifics, not abstractions
- **No rhyme chasing** — slant rhyme or no rhyme. Forced rhyme breaks the conversational spell
- **Lines under 10 syllables** — keeps the unhurried pace, prevents rushing
- **Chorus as reflection** — the chorus isn't a hook, it's the moral. The verse tells what happened, the chorus tells what it meant
- **Humming for outros** — "Mmm... mmm... mmm..." lets the song fade like the story trailing off

### Line examples
```
There was a road I used to walk
Before the houses filled the hill
The kind of road that doesn't end
It just gets quiet... and you keep going still
```

```
You don't remember the whole thing...
Just the light... and who was there
```

## Best Paired With

| Genre | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Folk / acoustic | Fingerpicked guitar matches the intimate delivery |
| Americana | Upright bass and gentle percussion support the warmth |
| Lo-fi | Vinyl crackle adds nostalgic texture to the storytelling |
| Indie folk | Light arrangement keeps focus on the voice |

## Advanced Settings

| Setting | Value | Reason |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Weirdness | 25-35% | Voice needs to stay warm and natural, not experimental |
| Style Influence | 60-70% | High enough to lock the gentle delivery, not so high it becomes monotone |
