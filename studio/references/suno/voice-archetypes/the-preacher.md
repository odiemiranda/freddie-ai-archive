# The Preacher

> Gospel-soul baritone — sermon energy, measured to fervent, raw conviction that lifts the room

---

## Voice Profile

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Register** | Baritone to tenor range (builds upward) |
| **Tone** | Warm, soulful, raw conviction |
| **Delivery** | Measured in verses, fervent in chorus, building intensity |
| **Energy** | Controlled crescendo — starts grounded, ends on fire |
| **Pacing** | Deliberate in verses, accelerating through pre-chorus, explosive on chorus |

## Style Tags

```
baritone male vocals, passionate sermon delivery, building from measured to fervent, soulful and spiritual, raw conviction
```

## Exclude Style

```
electronic, synth, auto-tune, female lead vocals, distortion, rock, metal, whispered, fast tempo
```

## Lyrics Technique

### Vocal direction tags (use before specific lines)
```
[Baritone, Measured, Sermon-like, Controlled]           ← verses, grounded opening
[Building, Rising conviction]                            ← pre-chorus, the ramp up
[Powerful vocals, Fervent, Choir harmonies behind]       ← chorus, full conviction
[Spoken word, Baritone, Intimate, Organ only]            ← bridge, quiet truth
[Full band, Choir, Climax, Fervent]                      ← final chorus, peak
[Softer, Choir hums, Organ fading]                       ← outro, gentle landing
```

### Writing rules
- **Build, don't start loud** — verses are measured and grounded. The power earns its way to the chorus through the pre-chorus ramp
- **ALL CAPS for conviction peaks** — "RISE!" "YOU WERE BUILT FOR THIS!" — sparingly, only on the chorus hook and final chorus
- **Ellipses for sermon pauses** — "Not yet... not you" — the Preacher pauses for effect, letting truth land
- **Concrete before abstract** — "I've counted every crack" before "something pulled me back." Ground the spiritual in the physical
- **Scars and survival imagery** — this voice talks about overcoming, not arriving. The journey matters more than the destination
- **Pre-chorus as the ramp** — use `[Building]` tags here. The pre-chorus is where measured becomes passionate
- **Choir as echo** — parenthesized text for choir responses: "(built for this...)" — Suno renders as backing vocals
- **Bridge as quiet confession** — strip to spoken word + organ. The most intimate moment before the final explosion
- **Short punchy chorus lines** — 5-7 syllables max. Conviction needs punch, not poetry

### Line examples
```
I've seen the bottom of the well
I've counted every crack
And when I thought the rope was gone
Something pulled me back
```

```
I'm not asking you to believe me...
I'm asking you to believe yourself
That's harder... I know
But you've done harder things
```

## Best Paired With

| Genre | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Gospel / soul | Hammond organ + choir is the natural home |
| Rock anthem | Power chords support the fervent delivery |
| Spoken word over builds | Instrumental builds behind the sermon |
| Cinematic inspirational | Strings and brass amplify the conviction |

## Advanced Settings

| Setting | Value | Reason |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Weirdness | 30-40% | Needs room for dynamic range between quiet verse and explosive chorus |
| Style Influence | 60-70% | Balanced — too high locks the quiet, too low loses the fervent peak |

## Tested

- 2026-03-21: Gospel soul, C Major, 96 BPM — measured-to-fervent build confirmed. Verses stayed grounded, pre-chorus ramped naturally, chorus hit full conviction. Spoken word bridge with organ created intimate contrast. Choir backing on parenthesized text worked as echo. ALL CAPS pushed Suno to shout delivery on "RISE!"
