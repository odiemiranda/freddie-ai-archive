### Style Block Notes

**Charcount:** 177 chars (target ~250-350 for vocal, hard max 500). Lean and focused.

**Slot validation:**
- Slot 2 (Genre): Cinematic folk -- cinematic-forward lean vs V1's "Epic folk"
- Slot 3 (Lead): baritone male vocals gravelly -- same vocal character
- Slot 3b (Arrangement): group chanting -- vocal arrangement
- Slot 4 (Support): cello sweeping phrases -- specific instrument (revised from "low strings" per tri-critic consensus to avoid violin-family ambiguity)
- Slot 5 (Rhythm): bodhrán steady pulse -- "steady" vs V1's "stomping" for a wider, more cinematic feel
- Slot 7 (Key/BPM): D Minor, 78 BPM -- same anchors
- Slot 9 (Mood): ancient, spacious -- "spacious" replaces V1's "earthy" for wider room feel (Space category vs Texture)
- Slot 10 (Production): clean mix, separated instruments -- mandatory

**Tri-critic summary:** Gemini 1 pass, MiniMax 1 pass, Grok 1 pass. 8 flags total. Accepted 1: changed "low strings sweeping phrases" to "cello sweeping phrases" (all 3 flagged vagueness/violin contradiction). Rejected 5 (genre rename, vocal header consolidation, Pre-Chorus replacement, bodhrán/stomp clash). Deferred 1 (tag density). Key consensus: all three praised exclude list discipline and structure tag reliability choices.

**Exclude rationale (14 items):** Same instrument blocks as V1. Swaps modern drums/hi-hat/snare for trumpet/french horn/timpani -- V2's cinematic lean risks Suno adding brass and orchestral percussion. Blocking those specifically prevents the cinematic genre from pulling in full orchestra.

**W/SI justification:** W:40% gives the cello slightly more room to interpret its sweeping phrases -- cinematic folk benefits from a touch more melodic freedom than the tight tavern stomp of V1. SI:65% still locks the group chanting character but allows Suno to lean into the cinematic production quality. Both within The Forge archetype tested range.
