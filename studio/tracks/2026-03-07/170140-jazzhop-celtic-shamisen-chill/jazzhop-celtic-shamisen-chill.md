1. **Nobody Asked What Time It Was** — Classic jazzhop foundation in B♭ Major. Harp carries smooth jazz chords over brush drums and walking bass. Shamisen and whistle float in and out. The most traditional jazzhop feel.
2. **The Playlist Chose Itself** — Shamisen and low whistle groove together over a swinging pocket. More fusion-forward, with the instruments playing off each other. Groovy and slightly playful.
3. **Somewhere Between Coffee and Dinner** — Same palette but in E♭ Major for a brighter, more urban feel. Harp leads with lifted chord voicings, whistle melody is lighter. The sunniest of the three.

All three use brush drums and walking bass instead of boom-bap — this gives the float and swing that separates jazzhop from lo-fi hip hop.

- 85 BPM is the jazzhop sweet spot — slower than boom-bap, enough swing to groove

- B♭ Major = warm and sophisticated; E♭ Major = brighter and more urban. Try both
