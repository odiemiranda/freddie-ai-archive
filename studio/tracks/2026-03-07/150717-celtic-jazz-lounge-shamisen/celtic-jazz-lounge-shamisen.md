## Variation Summary

1. **The Innkeeper's Monocle** — Irish harp leads with warm jazz chord voicings while the low whistle carries a gentle melody above. Bodhrán keeps soft time, shamisen plucks punctuate between phrases. The most structured and lounge-ready variation.
2. **Thistles in the Tip Jar** — Bodhrán takes a more prominent rhythmic role with a gentle bossa-jazz swing. Harp comps underneath while the low whistle and shamisen trade short melodic ideas. Lightest and most playful of the three.
3. **A Longer Way to Say Goodnight** — Most spacious and study-friendly. Low whistle takes long, breathy phrases over sustained harp chords and minimal bodhrán. Shamisen accents are at their most sparse. Best for deep work and programming.

All three use B♭ Major at 85 BPM. Entirely organic instrument palette — no synths, no electric instruments. The jazz feel comes from voicings and rhythm, not tone.

## Production Notes

- Irish harp should sound plucked and warm, not bright or classical — think jazz guitar voicings on strings
- Low whistle (not tin whistle) — breathy and warm in the lower register. If Suno outputs something piercing, regenerate
- Bodhrán should be gentle, almost like a hand drum — not the aggressive festival bodhrán
- Shamisen stays muted and occasional — one pluck every 4-8 bars at most
- Walking bass is the glue — if it disappears the track will lose its jazz foundation
- Run each 4-8 times — this is an unusual instrument combo so iteration matters
- If Suno struggles with "Irish harp", try "Celtic harp" or just "harp, warm plucked strings"
