## Variation Summary

1. **Someone Saved Us a Table** — Pure soundscape with no instruments. Crowd murmur, mug clinking, fire crackling, and the occasional laugh. Closest to a real pub recording.
2. **We Stayed Longer Than We Planned** — Tavern ambience layered with a barely-there acoustic guitar and soft hand percussion. Music sits underneath the scene, never above it.
3. **The Corner Booth Was Empty** — Medieval/fantasy tavern with gentle lute, crackling hearth, and deeper crowd texture. Great for D&D sessions, fantasy writing, or cozy immersion.

All three keep the social warmth front and center — the ambience should feel like being *in* the room, not listening to a recording of it.
