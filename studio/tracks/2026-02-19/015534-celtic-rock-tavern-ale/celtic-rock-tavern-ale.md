## Cultural Notes

### Key Instruments
- **Bodhrán**: Irish frame drum — the heartbeat of Celtic rock, played with a tipper for driving rhythms
- **Fiddle**: The melodic backbone, played aggressively for rock energy (not gentle classical violin)
- **Tin whistle**: High, piercing melody that cuts through heavy arrangements
- **Uilleann pipes**: Irish bellows-blown bagpipes, more expressive than Scottish highland pipes
- **Mandolin/Bouzouki**: Rapid tremolo picking adds Celtic rock's signature jangle

### Authentic Elements
- Celtic rock thrives on **call-and-response** between singer and crowd
- Drinking songs use **simple, repeatable choruses** designed for pub singalongs
- Deep male vocals in this style are **gravelly and raw**, not polished — think barroom not studio
- The rhythm should feel like **stomping boots and banging tankards**
- Lyrics celebrate **loyalty, drink, and companionship** with warmth and humor

### Key Artists for Reference
- **Dropkick Murphys**: Punk energy + Celtic instruments, Boston Irish-American rowdiness
- **The Pogues**: Shane MacGowan's raw vocals, London-Irish folk punk, poetic and messy
- **Flogging Molly**: Dave King's Irish brogue, violin-driven, pub-meets-punk
