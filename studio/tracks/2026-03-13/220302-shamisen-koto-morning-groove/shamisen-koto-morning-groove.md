## Instrument Notes

### Shamisen
- **Character**: Percussive plucked string, bright attack with sharp transient. The "bachi" striking technique gives it rhythmic punch that pairs naturally with hip hop
- **Best for**: Groovy, rhythmic passages — its attack cuts through a mix like a muted guitar
- **Pairs well with**: Boom-bap drums (similar percussive energy), upright bass, Rhodes
- **Avoid**: Too many competing plucked instruments — shamisen + acoustic guitar = mud

### Koto
- **Character**: Sweeping arpeggios, delicate melodic lines. More sustained and flowing than shamisen
- **Best for**: Melodic leads, gentle arpeggiated chords, atmospheric fills
- **Pairs well with**: Soft drums, warm pads, piano/Rhodes (complementary sustain)
- **Avoid**: Bright synths — koto's overtones clash with digital brightness

### Sonic Tips
- For morning coffee vibe, keep both instruments "warm" or "soft" — avoids the sharp traditional edge
- Shamisen's percussive attack makes it a natural rhythm instrument over hip hop beats
- Koto works better as a melodic voice, shamisen as rhythmic texture — playing to each instrument's strength
- Morning groove sits at 88-95 BPM: faster than lo-fi (75-85) but slower than full morning energy (110+)
- Major keys (G Major, D Major) give the morning brightness; A Minor adds contemplative depth without sadness
