[Intro]
[Fiddle melody, bodhrán pulse, crackling fire ambience]

[Verse 1]
[Male baritone, warm, storytelling]
Slammed the door on a sideways rain
Soot still black beneath my nails
The barkeep didn't ask my name
Just set the copper down and poured

[Verse 2]
[Building slightly, mandolin enters]
The forge smoke's barely off my coat
The hearth spits back what the storm blew in
Three days hammering someone else's blade
Now the only steel I want is a road

[Pre-Chorus]
[Bodhrán picks up, building tension]
But the sky won't quit, and the mud won't dry
So I'll sit where the fire still knows my kind

[Chorus]
[Full band, fiddle and tin whistle, driving]
One more mile and I'll smell the chimney
One more ridge and I'll see the gate
The mountain's had me long enough now
I'm bringing my hammer home

[Verse 3]
[Quieter, intimate, acoustic guitar]
The window's fogged from all this breathing
Strangers trading someone else's war
I trace a line across the glass --
That's north, that's where the lanterns are

[Verse 4]
[Rising energy, percussion builds]
The foam's gone flat, the fire's banking
A crack of blue between the clouds
The barkeep nods like he's seen this before --
A dwarf who's done with sitting down

[Chorus]
[Bigger, more voices join]
One more mile and I'll smell the chimney
One more ridge and I'll see the gate
The mountain's had me long enough now
I'm bringing my hammer home

when it's not your own

but never close enough
