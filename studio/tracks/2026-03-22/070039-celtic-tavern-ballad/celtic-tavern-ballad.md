## Production Notes

- **Bodhran is the only rhythm source** — no kit drums, no additional percussion. Let it breathe as a heartbeat, not a backbeat
- **Celtic harp sits in the high register** — keeps it out of the baritone vocal range (1-4 kHz). Arpeggios and sustains, not melodic leads
- **D Minor at 75 BPM** lands in the slow march zone — too fast feels like a reel, too slow loses the stomp. 75 is the sweet spot for a weary ballad that still has forward motion
- **Forge chanting needs stomping rhythm in the writing** — lines lock to a 4-beat foot-tap pattern. If it doesn't stomp, rewrite it
- **Commander spoken word needs short lines** — under 8 syllables, dramatic pauses via ellipses. Long sentences kill the gravelly authority
- **Exclude Style is critical** — block electric guitar, synth, electronic, pop, and female vocals aggressively. Celtic folk + deep baritone is a narrow lane and Suno will drift if not fenced
- **Run each variation 4-8 times** — the chanting/spoken word balance is unpredictable. Best results come from multiple generations
- **Gemini Review**: Skipped — straightforward arrangement with 2 instruments + bodhran, no frequency conflict risk. Celtic folk is well-documented in references
