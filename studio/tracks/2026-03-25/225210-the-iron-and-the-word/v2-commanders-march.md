# V2: Commander's March

> Sparse, spoken word is the centerpiece. Choruses erupt from near-silence.

---

### Track Names

1. Counting Steps — action fragment, the confession that defines this variation
2. Stand and Hold — fragment, the Commander's two orders before cracking
3. Forty Men — object as title, military headcount, implies loss
4. Before the Frost — fragment, departure without ceremony
5. Spoke Her Name — action fragment, reverence toward the mountain
6. Felt Right — fragment, the quiet mercy from verse 2
7. Coals from the Night — object as title, warmth surviving darkness
8. Maybe Less — fragment, Commander's uncertainty, two words that carry weight

### Lyrics

[Intro]
[Group humming, Deep, Ancient]
Hmm... hmm... hmm...
Hmm... hmm... hmm...

[Verse 1]
[Spoken word, Deep baritone, Authoritative, Gravelly]
We left before the frost...
Forty men. Maybe less.
The road was mud and stone.
No one spoke about the rest.

The smithy at the crossroads...
Still had coals from the night.

We didn't wake him. Felt right.

[Pre-Chorus]
[Building, Group chanting joins, Deeper]
Down... down... the valley road
Down... where the grey stone stands

[Chorus]
[Powerful chanting, Full group vocals, War drums pounding]
We forged the iron 'fore the sun
We hammered till the dawn was won
The mountain heard us speak her name
And NO man left the way he came
SING! SING! SING!

[Bridge]
[Spoken word, Deep baritone, Stripped back, Intimate]

[Full chanting, Maximum power, Stomping, Ancient]

THE MOUNTAIN HEARD US SPEAK HER NAME

SING! SING! SING!

[Outro]

### Production Notes
- Commander owns this version — spoken word is the star, everything else serves it
- Verses should feel almost solo — bodhrán pulse barely there, acoustic guitar fingerpicking only
- War drums and group chanting enter ONLY on choruses — the contrast is the point
- Higher Style Influence (70%) locks the deep baritone character more tightly
- Lower Weirdness (30%) keeps the Commander's delivery controlled and measured
- Bridge should be the most stripped moment in the entire track — near silence before the confession
