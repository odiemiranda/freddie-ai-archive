### Style

```
Celtic folk ballad, 85 BPM, D minor key, stripped back, near a cappella, intimate and vulnerable. Deep gravelly male baritone, close whispered delivery like confessing to the last man still awake by the dying fire, breath and room tone audible, raw and unpolished. Group male humming on choruses — gentle not rowdy, voices blending into warm sustained tones, ooh and mmm syllables layered with the lyric, reverent not aggressive. Single bodhrán heartbeat pulse barely audible underneath, more felt than heard. No guitar, no drums, no brass — voice and humming only with the faintest bodhrán. Natural room ambience, small stone room with embers, intimate and close. Verses are near-whispered solo voice, choruses swell gently with group humming harmonies underneath the lead, bridge is the most exposed vulnerable moment — solo voice cracking slightly on the confession. Final chorus adds the most voices but stays restrained, a quiet collective acknowledgment not a battle cry. The song should feel like the last story told before sleep, after the battle is over and the fire is almost out.
```
