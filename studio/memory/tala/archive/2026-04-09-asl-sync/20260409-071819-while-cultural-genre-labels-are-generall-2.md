## Observation
Mirroring a source Style block containing a cultural genre label ('Celtic neofolk dirge') and a high number of descriptive elements (16) in a `create-track-variant` workflow resulted in a track that 'worked' but was 'monotone'.

## Learning
While cultural genre labels are generally discouraged for new prompts, mirroring them from a *proven source prompt* in a variant workflow might not lead to outright failure, but can still introduce subtle quality issues like monotony. Additionally, a high count of descriptive elements, even if within character budget, can contribute to a lack of dynamic range or monotony, reinforcing the need for lean prompts.

## Evidence
Gen 5 rebuilt mirroring source Style (Celtic neofolk dirge, named instruments with verbs, zero negatives, 16 descriptive elements). Worked but monotone.
