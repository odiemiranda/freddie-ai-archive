Using "celtic harp" in a Style block did NOT bleed extra celtic instruments (tin whistle, fiddle, flute) when the Exclude list explicitly bans them. Tested with 12-item Exclude including tin whistle, fiddle, flute.

**Why:** The broad Exclude list (10-12 items) acts as a safety net. "Celtic" is a risk word that could trigger associated instruments, but the Exclude catches them.

**How to apply:** When using "celtic harp" or similar cultural instrument labels, always exclude the common associated instruments from that tradition. If bleed occurs on future generations, fall back to neutral labels: "folk harp" or just "harp" with descriptive arpeggios doing the work.
