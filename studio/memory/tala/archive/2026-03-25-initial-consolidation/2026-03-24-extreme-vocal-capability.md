Suno can simulate advanced vocal mechanics (microtonal slides, vocal cord flips, two-tone overtones) but has a hard melodic bias — it cannot produce pure non-melodic vocalization.

**What works (tested, W:10% SI:85%):**
- Carnatic / Indian classical — gamaka ornaments, microtonal slides
- Opera / bel canto — wide vibrato, diaphragm projection
- Yodeling — chest-to-falsetto crack, vocal cord flip
- Jazz scat — nonsense syllables as instrumental phrases
- Whistle register — piercing high-frequency zipped-cord tone
- Overtone / throat singing — simultaneous deep drone + high harmonic

**What fails:**
- Haka / non-melodic chanting — Suno adds melody regardless of prompting. Hard limitation.
- Any pure rhythmic shouting, spoken aggression, or non-musical vocalization gets "musicalized"

**Key rule:** Low weirdness (~10%) + high style influence (~85%) for all extreme techniques. High weirdness adds creative deviations that break the technique's authenticity.

**Combine with range headers:** Throat singing → C2-E3. Whistle register → C5-C6. Opera → full range + exclude pop/breathy/intimate.
