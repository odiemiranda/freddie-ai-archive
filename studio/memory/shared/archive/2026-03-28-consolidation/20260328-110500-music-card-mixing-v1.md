## Music Card Mixing Test: Shamisen-Jazz + Celtic Folk Subversion

**Cards used as anchors:**
- Shamisen Jazz Cafe (92.3 BPM, G minor) — groove, shamisen plucking, brushed drums
- Japan Cafe Light Jazz (80.7 BPM, Bb major) — warmth, intimacy, walking bass
- Coffee Lofi (~80 BPM) — morning texture, lazy swing

**Subversion:** Replaced Tokyo night/cafe identity with morning fireside. Shamisen as melodic counterpoint to celtic harp instead of saxophone. Jazz harmony stays, mood shifts to pastoral warmth.

**Results at W:30 / SI:70:**
1. Cards anchor warmth/tempo/texture effectively — typed brackets handle the fusion twist
2. Shamisen + celtic harp pairing works when both constrained to mid-low register (no high notes)
3. F Major at 78 BPM = warm pastoral morning sweet spot
4. Pipe vs stacked [Instrument:] syntax = same DNA at this W/SI setting, pipe is cleaner
5. Fiddle emerged from "folk jazz" Style tag genre gravity even without explicit tagging — controlled, not dominant at SI:70
6. Empty Exclude Style worked — typed brackets provided sufficient instrument control
