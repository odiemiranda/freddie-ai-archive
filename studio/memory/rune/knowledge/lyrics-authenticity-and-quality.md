## Overview
Authenticity and originality in lyrics are paramount. Rune must ensure content is genuine, avoids fabrication, and is free from generic "AI-slop" or clichés, especially when incorporating non-English languages or regional dialects. Listener trust and emotional impact depend on truthful and unique expression.

## Non-English Vocabulary and Slang
- **Never Fabricate:** Do not invent or distort words to force a rhyme (e.g., Rune's "sayin" error in Tagalog).
- **Verify Realness:** When writing in any language outside English, verify words are conversational and real. If unsure, restructure the line.
- **Rhyme Strategy:** Prefer slant rhymes or rewrites over inventing vocabulary. Authenticity is worth more than a perfect rhyme.
- **Conversational Focus:** Prefer common, conversational words that sound natural when spoken/sung.

## Brand Names and References
- **Avoid Brands:** Do not use brand names (e.g., Jollibee, Starbucks). Use generic references instead.
- **Why:** Generic references age better, avoid legal/brand association issues, and keep the focus on the emotion rather than the commercial product.

## AI-Slop and Cliché Detection
An integrated AI-slop detection system scans lyrics for generic, unoriginal, or emotionally flat content.
- **Checks Performed:** Scans for universal AI-slop, emotional flatteners, romantic clichés, and genre-specific clichés.
- **Context is Key:** The system reports cliché counts but requires Rune's context to distinguish intentional, core conceits from actual clichés.
- **Concrete Imagery:** Writing with concrete imagery from the start significantly reduces the likelihood of triggering slop detection.

## Why
- **Listener Trust:** Fabricated words or generic content break listener trust and ruin emotional impact.
- **Timelessness:** Avoiding brands and clichés ensures lyrics remain relevant and impactful over time.
- **Originality:** Proactive quality checks ensure the output is unique and stands out.
- **Efficiency:** Catching "slop" early reduces revisions and improves overall output quality.

## How to Apply
1.  **The Overheard Test:** Could this line be heard spoken naturally in a local market or on a bus?
2.  **Restructure First:** If a rhyme feels "expensive" (requiring a rare or questionable word), change the preceding line to find a more natural path.
3.  **Draft Standard First:** Ensure the meaning is clear in standard form before adjusting for dialect or regional slang.
4.  **Apply IMAGE TEST:** During writing, prioritize concrete imagery to naturally avoid generic phrasing.
5.  **Review Slop Reports:** Pay attention to AI-slop detection reports in Style Block Notes as a safety net, using context to evaluate flagged items.
