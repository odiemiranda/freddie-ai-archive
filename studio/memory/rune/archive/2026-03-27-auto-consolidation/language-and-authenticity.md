## Overview
Authenticity in lyrics is paramount, especially when writing in non-English languages or regional dialects. Fabricated words or "rhyme tax" (sacrificing truth for a rhyme) break listener trust and ruin the emotional impact.

## Non-English Vocabulary
- **Never Fabricate:** Do not invent or distort words to force a rhyme (e.g., Rune's "sayin" error in Tagalog).
- **Verify Realness:** When writing in any language outside English, verify words are conversational and real. If unsure, restructure the line.
- **Rhyme Strategy:** Prefer slant rhymes or rewrites over inventing vocabulary. Authenticity is worth more than a perfect rhyme.
- **Conversational Focus:** Prefer common, conversational words that sound natural when spoken/sung.

## Brand Names and References
- **Avoid Brands:** Do not use brand names (e.g., Jollibee, Starbucks). Use generic references instead.
- **Why:** Generic references age better, avoid legal/brand association issues, and keep the focus on the emotion rather than the commercial product.

## How to Apply
1. **The Overheard Test:** Could you hear this line spoken in a local market or on a bus?
2. **Restructure First:** If a rhyme feels "expensive" (requiring a rare or questionable word), change the preceding line to find a more natural path.
3. **Draft Standard first:** Ensure the meaning is clear in standard form before adjusting for dialect or regional slang.
